Muglers New Creative Director To Begin April 1st

In 1973 Thierry Mugler debuted his first collection. 50+ years later and Mugler is a fashion powerhouse known for their unique designs different from many other fashion houses. Despite leaving fashion in 2002 Thierry Mugler has continued to design costumes for circ du solei and famously designed Kim Kardashians 2019 ‘Wet’ dress for the Met gala. Mugler has changed creative directors multiple times since Thierry left in 2002, most recently Miguel Castro Freitas will be taking over from Casey Cadwallader.

Miguel Castro Freitas

This April 1st Miguel Castro Freitas is stepping into the role of creative director at Mugler. A former dancer in his youth, Freitas' artistic background echoes that of the house's founder, Manfred Thierry Mugler. He graduated from Central Saint Martins in London in 2004, having studied in the womenswear program, and went on to hold senior design positions at prestigious fashion houses such as Sportmax, Dries Van Noten, and Christian Dior Couture. Reflecting on his appointment, Freitas expressed that, "It is an honor to join the spectacular house of Mugler. As one of the 20th century's great couturiers, Mr. Mugler reimagined the power and limits of fashion. Alongside the teams, I am thrilled to bring my own vision, story, and emotion to this monumental heritage." Mugler's first collection under his supervision will debut in Paris fashion week this coming fall and Freitas is expected to bring a storytelling approach to his work, blending narrative with design in new, exciting ways.

Wicked On The Red Carpet

One of the biggest Films of 2024, the musical Wicked is revered for its musical numbers, phenomenal acting, and ability to transport the viewer to the Land Of Oz. However my attention through the film was on the magnificent costumes; because of this, it came as no surprise that the red carpet looks worn to the Oscars by the Wicked stars were just as incredible.

Ariana Grande

Schiaparelli

Glinda made her entrance in a floating bubble, and Ariana Grandes dress at the Oscars was just as memorable. The Schiaparelli gown the actress and singer wore was fresh of the runway being shown in Paris just a few weeks prior. The gown was customized in a light pink for Ariana and featured a skirt made with more than 190,000 crystal sequins, rhinestones, and cut beads.

Cynthia Ervo

Louis Vitton

Ervo Arrived at the Oscars in a stunning, custom, dark green velvet ballgown from Louis Vuitton. This dress and its dramatic silhouette and pointy collar was very fitting for her character Elphaba in Wicked. Cynthia shared that the dress was meant to be a homage to legendary actress Hattie McDaniel; the first black woman to win an Oscar.
